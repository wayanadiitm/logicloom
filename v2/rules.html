Guide Book

Rules and Instructions

There will be a total of 15 MCQs (single correct option type) in the Prelims (Creativity round). The marking scheme will be as follows: +3 (for each correct response), 0 (for wrong answer) and +1 (for every skipped question). So, the maximum points one can achieve is 45. This will be an individual participation round.

The prelims will be a timed quiz of 30 mins duration with a flexible attempt window. One can participate at any convenient time through out the day. It will be hosted on our dedicated event portal. There won't be any specific time constraint for a question. Once started, the submission has to be done within half an hour from the start time.

Ensure that you've a stable internet connection. High bandwidth or a lot of mobile data requirement isn't needed though. A decent connectivity speed should suffice.

A timer would be visible on the portal. This might differ from what's showing on your system. Please cross-check the server time. There will be 2 buttons available - Save and Submit. Submit is a one-step final process and once that is clicked, no further modifications will be allowed. The Save feature is extremely useful and we suggest you to keep saving your responses intermittently. If you get disconnected, the answers would be auto-saved at the backend and you can safely resume from where you left. Unfortunately, we won't adjust the lost time in this case. You can keep changing your responses until you submit.

The responses last saved or marked during submission will be considered and auto-graded by the system. Incase the timer comes to an end and you miss pressing Submit, there's nothing to worry, provided you are on the portal. Auto-submission has been enabled.

You are not allowed to navigate to any other screen while the quiz is ongoing, unless stated for any question (we will allow for the creativity round only, as we believe online learning might be needed here). Various security tools have been implemented in the portal to track any cases of UFM (like, but not limited to, multiple logins on more than one device, long away time, multiple logouts etc.). All activity logs will be processed in an automated way and any such cases will lead to direct cancellation of the participation status. As a student of a reputed institute like IITM, you are responsible enough to adhere to the contest CoC, be honest and maintain the integrity.

The portal is responsive and can be accessed on laptop/PC/mobile. However a PC/laptop is preferred for a better web experience. You would need to disable your anti-virus alerts/notifications from the settings. Similarly incase of android, keep the phone silent and turn on the DND mode during the contest. Also, Chrome/Edge browser is preferred and Incognito mode will be the best to ensure that there're no stored cache issues. Due to a huge server load, there may be some issues like repeated loading of screen. Click on the Refresh icon on the portal in such cases.

We will check the relative performance and hence, the raw points obtained will be converted to percentile score. A cut-off point will be decided taking into consideration the difficulty factor and whole analytics and the top N participants from each category (FL and DD) will qualify for the pre-finals. Since we've another round, we will try to keep the qualifying score decent enough and filter accordingly.

Qualified participants will be randomly assigned to teams. However the allocation would be balanced wrt. equal number of students from both categories in a team. A total of 20 questions need to be solved in 1 hour. This will be a collaborative round, members can discuss amongst themselves via GMeet, video call etc. Only the team leader will be able to answer and submit from the portal. Each correct answer yields 5 points and guess what, there's no negative marking here!

We have an interesting workshop and a challenging finale that would be conducted at campus.

Prize Eligibility: Students need to register for LogicLooM 2.0 via student email id on IITM Paradox portal and attempt atleast the prelims (adhering to the CoC) to be eligible for a Participation Certificate. Participants who qualify for the pre-finals will receive the 'Appreciation Certificate'. Finalists will be awarded with a 'Achievement Certificate'. The top 3 teams from the finale (winner, first and second runner-up) will be awarded with the 'Accomplishment Certificate' at campus.

Incase of any technical issues, feel free to drop an email at logicloom@ds.study.iitm.ac.in. We will try our best to respond and solve the issue as quick as possible.
